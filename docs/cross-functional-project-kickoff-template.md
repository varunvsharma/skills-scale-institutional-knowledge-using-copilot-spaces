# Cross-Functional Project Kickoff Template

## Purpose
This template helps project teams conduct an effective kickoff meeting that aligns all cross-functional roles on goals, responsibilities, communication, and success criteria.

## When to Use
- At the start of any project involving multiple roles or teams
- When forming a new project team
- For major feature releases or initiatives requiring coordination across functions

---

## Pre-Kickoff Preparation
**Owner: Project Manager**

- [ ] Confirm all key roles are identified and assigned
- [ ] Draft initial project charter or one-pager
- [ ] Identify key stakeholders and decision-makers
- [ ] Schedule kickoff meeting with all roles represented
- [ ] Share pre-read materials 48 hours in advance

---

## Kickoff Meeting Agenda

### 1. Introductions & Roles (10 min)
**Facilitated by: Project Manager**

- Team introductions: name, role, and primary focus area
- Clarify each role's scope and key responsibilities on this project
- Identify role representatives who will serve as primary points of contact

**Roles Typically Present:**
- Project Manager
- Product Manager
- Tech Lead or Senior Developer
- UX Designer (if applicable)
- Business Analyst (if applicable)
- QA Automation Engineer (if applicable)
- Release Manager (for release planning)
- Support Lead (for customer impact visibility)

---

### 2. Project Context & Goals (15 min)
**Presented by: Product Manager**

- **Problem Statement**: What customer or business problem are we solving?
- **Success Criteria**: How will we measure success? (OKRs, metrics, KPIs)
- **Scope**: What's in scope and explicitly out of scope?
- **Constraints**: Timeline, budget, dependencies, or regulatory requirements

**Discussion Points:**
- Does everyone understand the "why" behind this project?
- Are success metrics clear and measurable?
- Are there any open questions about scope?

---

### 3. Technical Approach & Architecture (10 min)
**Presented by: Tech Lead/Senior Developer**

- High-level technical approach or architecture
- Key technical decisions or patterns to be used
- Integration points and external dependencies
- Technical risks and mitigations

**Discussion Points:**
- Are there any design considerations needed from UX?
- What are the testability requirements?
- Are there any concerns about feasibility or timelines?

---

### 4. Role-Specific Contributions (20 min)
**Facilitated by: Project Manager**

Go around the table and have each role describe:
- **What I'm responsible for**: Key deliverables and activities
- **What I need from other roles**: Dependencies and inputs
- **What I'll provide to other roles**: Outputs and handoffs
- **My biggest concerns or risks**: What keeps me up at night

**Key Roles to Cover:**
- **UX Designer**: Research, design deliverables, design reviews
- **Business Analyst**: Requirements documentation, process flows, acceptance criteria
- **Developers**: Implementation, code reviews, technical documentation
- **QA Automation Engineer**: Test strategy, automation coverage, quality gates
- **Release Manager**: Release timeline, deployment checklist, go-live criteria
- **Support Lead**: Support readiness, documentation needs, escalation paths

---

### 5. Communication & Collaboration Plan (10 min)
**Facilitated by: Project Manager**

- **Meeting cadence**: Standups, sprint planning, demos, retrospectives
- **Communication channels**: Slack/Teams channels, email lists, project boards
- **Documentation location**: Where do we store specs, decisions, and artifacts?
- **Decision-making process**: How do we escalate and resolve blockers?
- **Status reporting**: Frequency and format of progress updates

---

### 6. Timeline & Milestones (10 min)
**Presented by: Project Manager**

- Key milestones and target dates
- Sprint/iteration boundaries
- Release windows and deployment schedule
- Key decision points or gates

**Discussion Points:**
- Are milestones realistic given the scope and dependencies?
- Are there any conflicting priorities or resource constraints?
- What's our buffer for unexpected issues?

---

### 7. Risks & Dependencies (10 min)
**Facilitated by: Project Manager**

- Brainstorm known risks (technical, resource, timeline, external)
- Identify cross-team dependencies
- Assign risk owners and mitigation actions
- Document in the Risk Register

---

### 8. Action Items & Next Steps (5 min)
**Facilitated by: Project Manager**

- Summarize key action items with owners and due dates
- Confirm next meetings and sync points
- Share meeting notes and action items within 24 hours

---

## Post-Kickoff Activities

### Project Manager
- [ ] Distribute meeting notes and action items to all attendees
- [ ] Create or update project board with initial backlog
- [ ] Set up regular sync meetings and invite all stakeholders
- [ ] Establish Risk Register and begin tracking

### Product Manager
- [ ] Finalize product requirements and acceptance criteria
- [ ] Prioritize backlog items
- [ ] Schedule user research or validation sessions if needed

### UX Designer
- [ ] Create design timeline and key review points
- [ ] Begin user research or design exploration
- [ ] Share design files location with team

### Business Analyst
- [ ] Document detailed requirements and business rules
- [ ] Create process flows and workflow diagrams
- [ ] Review and refine acceptance criteria with Product Manager

### Developers
- [ ] Review technical approach and identify spikes or unknowns
- [ ] Estimate backlog items
- [ ] Set up development environment and repositories

### QA Automation Engineer
- [ ] Draft test strategy and coverage plan
- [ ] Identify testing tools and frameworks needed
- [ ] Define quality gates for releases

### Release Manager
- [ ] Create release plan and deployment checklist
- [ ] Identify release dependencies and coordination needs
- [ ] Set up release communication channels

### Support Lead
- [ ] Review customer impact and prepare support readiness plan
- [ ] Identify documentation and training needs
- [ ] Establish escalation and feedback channels

---

## Success Indicators for a Good Kickoff
- ✅ All roles understand project goals and their responsibilities
- ✅ Dependencies and handoffs between roles are clear
- ✅ Communication channels and meeting cadence are established
- ✅ Known risks are documented with owners assigned
- ✅ Everyone knows where to find information and how to escalate
- ✅ Team feels energized and aligned on the path forward
